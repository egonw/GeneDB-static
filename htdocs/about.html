Funding

The GeneDB website is funded by Wellcome through its core support of the Wellcome Sanger Institute. Curation of Plasmodium genomes is also supported via the Wellcome Sanger Institute.

Additional funding to Prof. Christiane Hertz-Fowler (University of Liverpool and EuPathDB; grants WT108443MA & 212929/Z/18/Z) supports curation of fungal and trypanosomatid reference genomes.

Curators

Plasmodium species:

Dr. Ulrike Böhme, Wellcome Sanger Institute

Ulrike curates and annotates the genomes of 11 Plasmodium species: the human malaria parasites P. falciparum, P. vivax, P. knowlesi, P. malariae and P. ovale; the rodent malaria parasites P. berghei, P. chabaudi, P. yoelii yoelii; the avian malaria species P. relictum and P. gallinaceum; and the chimpanzee-infective P. reichenowi.

For any curation requests please contact: ucb@sanger.ac.uk

Trypanosoma, Leishmania and fungal species:

Dr. David Starns, University of Liverpool

David curates and annotates genomes of fungal and kinetoplastid species from GeneDB in collaboration with EuPathDB, focusing on the automated integration of bulk data types.

For any curation requests please contact D.E.Starns@liverpool.ac.uk

Dr. Achchuthan Shanmugasundram, University of Liverpool

Achchuthan curates and annotates genomes of fungal and kinetoplastid species from GeneDB: Aspergillus fumigatus, A. nidulans, Coccidioides immitis, Cryptococcus neoformans, Neurospora crassa, Trypanosoma brucei, T. congolense, T. cruzi, T. vivax, Leishmania braziliensis, L. donovani, L. major and L. mexicana. He focuses mainly on the curation of gene-specific functional information for these genomes.

For any curation requests please contact: achchu@liverpool.ac.uk
